Theorem 1.6.5. Arithmetic properties of natural numbers.

All of the following are true for any integers \(x,y,z\text{.}\)
Additive identity
🔗
\(\displaystyle x + 0 = x\)
🔗
Associativity of addition
🔗
\(\displaystyle x + (y + z) = (x + y) + z\)
🔗
Commutativity of addition
🔗
\(\displaystyle x + y = y + x\)
🔗
Multiplicative identity
🔗
\(\displaystyle x \cdot 1 = x\)
🔗
Multiplicative annihilation
🔗
\(\displaystyle x \cdot 0 = 0\)
🔗
Associativity of multiplication
🔗
\(\displaystyle x \cdot (y \cdot z) = (x \cdot y) \cdot z\)
🔗
Commutativity of multiplication
🔗
\(\displaystyle x \cdot y = y \cdot x\)
🔗
Distributivity
🔗
\(\displaystyle x \cdot (y + z) = xy + xz\)
